Good and Evil in Harry Potter and Lord of the Rings

By Jessica Hardey


Overview:

This project seeks explore the concept of good and evil in Harry Potter and Lord of the Rings by analyzing the language used by characters in both series. In order to accomplish this, I used BookNLP, Python, Github, and Tableau. The below visualizations primarily explore three different ideas: the relative goodness of characters within a series, the change in character goodness across a series, and words in each series that were strong indicators of goodness or badness.


Character Goodness Rankings in Harry Potter and Lord of the Rings

The chart below shows the Harry Potter characters ranked by goodness in three different ways. The left-most chart has Snape, Madeye Moody, and Sirius Black categorized as good, the middle chart as those characters categorized as bad, and the last uses only a small set of good and bad characters as a training set in order to calculate scores for all the other characters, thereby predicting their "goodness" or "badness"

Most "Good" and "Bad" Words in Harry Potter and Lord of the Rings

The two charts below show the words that were most indicative of goodness and badness in Harry Potter and Lord of the Rings, respectively. The words shown below all had a frequency of greater than 20. This score was determined by taking the ratio of the word's frequency in language used by good characters over the frequency in language used by bad characters. The higher the ratio, the more indicative of "goodness".
